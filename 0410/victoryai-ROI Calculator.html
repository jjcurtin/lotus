01 — The Clinical Data Gap

Every day between visits is unbilled revenue.

For most MAT/OUD programs, the only documented patient data is what appears during scheduled encounters — typically 1–4 visits per month. Every daily MAT confirmation, craving log, CBT session, COWS score, or post-discharge check-in is a billable clinical event generating zero documentation and zero revenue. The codes below legally cannot be billed without that between-visit data — and until now, no tool existed to capture it systematically.

The specific thresholds being missed today

RTM 98980 requires ≥16 engagement days/month + one interactive communication. CCM 99490 requires ≥20 documented non-face-to-face minutes/month. G2214 requires ≥11 consecutive MAT days confirmed. TCM 99495/99496 requires a 2-day post-discharge contact on record. Without a between-visit digital platform, none of these thresholds are provable — and every one of these codes is unbillable.

04 — State-by-State Medicaid Rates

Same patient. Different revenue by state.

Medicaid rates are set by each state, typically 50–90% of the Medicare Physician Fee Schedule. A patient in Massachusetts generates ~57% more RTM + CCM revenue than the same patient in Texas — purely from fee schedule differences. Multi-state operators should prioritize digital documentation infrastructure in high-rate states first.

Key strategic insight

Massachusetts ($140/pt/mo RTM+CCM) and New York ($133/pt/mo) offer the strongest ROI for early adoption. California ($120/pt/mo) follows with additional CHW revenue available only in Medi-Cal. Texas and Florida ($89–100/pt/mo) are viable but require a higher commercial payer mix to reach breakeven at smaller patient panels.

05 — The Bigger Picture

Billing codes are the upside. Retention is the headline.

The Retention Math

40–60% of MAT patients drop out within 6 months (SAMHSA TIP 63). Each dropout represents $4,000–$8,000 in lost program revenue. A 10% improvement in 6-month retention across a 200-patient program preserves $80,000–$160,000/year — before a single billing code is optimized. The app drives both: better between-visit data enables billing, and better engagement reduces dropout.

Combined CFO Story

Billing code uplift at 200 patients: $192K–$480K/year. Retention preservation (10% improvement): $160K–$320K/year. DHCS/DMC-ODS contract protection from measurable outcomes: material but unquantified. Realistic Year 1 combined impact: $350K–$800K for a 200-patient program. Platform cost at scale: $60K–$120K/year.

Disclaimers, Methodology & Sources

Revenue figures are effective realized estimates, not theoretical maximums. All figures derived from the selected state Medicaid fee schedule blended with a commercial payer mix using the selected engagement realization rate. They assume code prerequisites are met for qualifying patients — not all codes stack simultaneously for all patients.

Billing prerequisites are absolute. RTM (98977/98980/98981) requires a QHCP order, a qualifying device/app, ≥16 days of monitored data, and at least one interactive communication per month. CCM (99490) and PCM (99424) cannot double-count time with RTM in the same billing period. CoCM (99492/99493) and BHI (99484) are mutually exclusive, require a defined collaborative care model with a psychiatric consultant, and are excluded from this model. G2213/G2214 require FDA-approved MAT in active use.

VictoryAI is not a billing service. VictoryAI captures structured engagement data and exports it to your EMR. Your billing team reviews all data, determines medical necessity, and submits claims. VictoryAI does not submit claims, guarantee reimbursement, or determine medical necessity. All claims are subject to payer contracts, prior authorization requirements, and patient eligibility.

Medi-Cal fragmentation. Medi-Cal coverage is fragmented across DMC-ODS and managed care plans (CalOptima, LA Care, Inland Empire Health Plan). Many digital health codes require prior authorization. RTM coverage varies by managed care plan. Confirm coverage and PA requirements with each plan before billing.
